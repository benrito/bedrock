Lightbeam for Firefox

See who’s tracking you online.

Shine a light on who’s watching you

Lightbeam is a Firefox add-on that allows you to see the third parties that are collecting information about your browsing activity, with and without your consent. Using interactive visualizations, Lightbeam shows you the relationships between these third parties and the sites you visit.
Download the Lightbeam add-on for Firefox
Download and run the Lightbeam add-on in your Firefox browser.
Lightbeam will create a de-identified record of events for every site you visit and every third party site that connects to you.
Lightbeam visually graphs these events to highlight the interactions between sites you intentionally visit and the third parties that load without your consent.
Lightbeam will continue to add to your graph as you browse the Web. You can stop Lightbeam at any time by disabling it or uninstalling the add-on. Your personal data can be easily saved or deleted.

How Lightbeam Works


Visualize your data

After you download and install the Lightbeam add-on to Firefox, it will begin watching connections — giving you interactive visualizations of your browsing activity and how that data is being collected.

Analyze your connections

As you view the visualizations, you can take a closer look into the websites and their third party connections that are stored in your data. You can also reset or save your data to create your own visualizations.

Contribute your data

To help us do further research around online tracking and privacy, you can donate your de-identified data to our Lightbeam database.

Tracking & privacy

Not all tracking is bad. Many services rely on user data to provide relevant content and enhance your online experience. But most tracking happens without users’ consent and without their knowledge. That’s not okay. It should be you who decides when, how and if you want your data to be shared. We recognize the importance of transparency and our mission is all about empowering users — both with tools and information. About Lightbeam »

Explore the database

In the Lightbeam database, you will be able to browse first and third-party connection data uploaded by de-identified donors who use the Lightbeam add-on. We’re building this crowd-sourced directory to encourage users to visualize the state of tracking as an aggregate and to examine relationships that exist on the Web. Go to database »
