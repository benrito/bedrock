Discover who is tracking you online

Lightbeam is an add-on for Firefox and allows you to see all the third parties that are tracking your movements across the Web. It will show, in real time, the interaction between companies and other trackers with various visualizaitons.
Download the Lightbeam add-on for Firefox
Demo Video
You download and run the Lightbeam add-on in your Firefox browser.
Whenever you visit a website, and that site notifies a third party, Lightbeam makes an de-identified record of this event.
Lightbeam visually graphs these events to highlight the interactions between sites you intentionally visit and the third parties that load without your consent.
Lightbeam will continue to add to your graph as you browse the web. You can stop Lightbeam at any time by disabling it or uninstalling the add-on. Your personal data can be easily saved or deleted.

How Lightbeam Works


Explore Visualization

Download the add-on to your Firefox browser. Open the add-on to see the various visualizations of your browsing being tracked by third parties.

Analyze the Trackers

As you view the visualizations that update in real-time, you can explore and analyze the website and connections that have connected to you that are stored in your data.

Contribute Data

You can reset or save your data as a .json file to make your own visualizations. You can donate your de-identified data to our database to further promote research in the field of online tracking and privacy.

Tracking & Privacy

Not all tracking is bad. Many services rely on user data to provide relevant content and enhance your online experience. But most tracking happens without users' consent and without their knowledge. That's not okay. It should be you who decides when , how and if you want to be tracked. We recognize the importance of transparency and our mission is all about empowering users -- both with tools and with information. >> Go to About
